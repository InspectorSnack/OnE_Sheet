        > Item Quality Tags
        Tag Description
        Scrap This was poorly crafted using scraps, and reduces durability each time this skill roll fails.
        Standard These are perfectly normal with no added or reduced effect.
        Professional This was made for professional use, these items grant +1 to skill rolls using this item.
        Masterwork This is a piece of art and constructed with the best materials. Grants +3 to skill rolls using this item, and improves reaction modifiers by 2 when appropriately shown off.
        > Melee Tags
        Tag Description
        Serrated Imparts a jagged edge that increases bleeding and tissue damage.
        Weighted Adds momentum to swings, increasing force and damage potential.
        > Ammo Tags
        Tag Description
        Subsonic These bullets travel below the speed of sound and do not produce a sonic crack as they fly. When paired with a silenced weapon, enemies will not hear a bullet pass by them. Subsonic ammo has a shorter effective range by half.
        Penetrating Designed to pierce through hardened targets, attacks made with this weapon ignore 2 DR and on a successful hit will always do minimum damage to the target regardless of DR.
        Overpressured Loaded with extra propellant, grants +2 DP at the cost of Disadvantage to hit.
        High Velocity High Velocity rounds are loaded more efficiently and the bullets cut through the air without losing as much energy.

        +2 to Range, Target gets 1D3 Disadvantage to Defend.
        Incendiary Incendiary rounds do only 1/2 normal damage, however targets struck by incendiary rounds have to roll against their HTH to avoid gaining the burning status effect.
        Hollowpoint Hollowpoint rounds are made to “squash” on impact, increasing internal damage and making medical aid more difficult.

        Target HP becomes Compromised, +2 to Target DR.
        Flechette Flechette shells are shotgun ammunition that are loaded with spikes and needles instead of beads. Flechette shells destroy composite armors and cause heavy bleeding on targets.

        -3 DP, Target receives bleeding status effect, Destroys composite armor.
        Slug Slugs are a shotgun ammo type that instead of firing a column of pellets at a target, is a single massive projectile.

        +2 DP, 1D3 DIS to hit.
        > Wyrd Tags
        Tag Description
        Lucky Reality bends to ensure this weapon finds its mark. Once per combat encounter, if you fail a Skill roll using this weapon, you may immediately reroll the 2D12 and take the new result.
        Persistent The weapon develops a hunger for a specific target once it tastes them. Each consecutive, successful hit against the same target grants a cumulative +1 to your Damage Potential (DP) for the next attack against them. If you miss a shot the bonus will reset.
        Bound This weapon is bound to its last wielder, and will violently rip towards them if not on their person; you must succeed a Dexterity Roll to catch the weapon when it is returning. If failed you will need to grab it yourself, if critically failed it will hit you and act as an attack.
        Gravitic This weapon is unaffected by local gravity, making it feel almost weightless. It ignores the Cumbersome tags and reduces overall weight by half, allowing heavy machine guns or sledgehammers to be wielded with ease.
        Whispering This weapon must be tied to a soul because it speaks to you, it can warn you of danger or guide you to or out of nearby anomalies. When in an anomaly you can choose to use the whispers instead of perception, however they may not always be reliable.
        Synchronic This weapon exists in a state of quantum superposition, striking across multiple realities at once. When you make an attack, roll a 1D3 Reality Die alongside your 2D12 Skill roll.

        1: The attack hits in all realities. If the target successfully dodges, or if you fail your Skill roll by 3 or less (a near miss), the attack still counts as a standard, full-damage hit.

        2: The attack hits in only half of reality. If the target dodges, or if you fail your Skill roll by 3 or less, the target still takes Half Damage (calculate standard damage against their armor, then halve the final result).

        3: The attack misses in most realities. If your 2D12 roll is a normal success, the attack only deals Half Damage. A missed roll remains a miss.

        Exception: A Critical Success overrides the Reality Die. The attack forces its way into reality and functions as a normal Critical Hit regardless of what the 1D3 shows.
        Lodestone This weapon exhibits anomalous magnetism that only affects metals. You gain Advantage on Ranged or Melee Skill rolls when targeting enemies wearing metallic armor.
        > Firearm Tags
        Tag Description
        Heavy Recoil This weapon really kicks, when firing multiple rounds in a turn the user must make a strength roll. If failed you will have disadvantage if you shoot during your next turn when using this weapon.
        Silent Eliminates the muzzle report of the firearm, firing this weapon while hidden will not reveal your position to enemies further than 3m away. Unless this weapon also has subsonic any enemy within 3m of the bullets flight path will hear the snap of a bullet passing by and know the shot was fired and the general direction it came from.
        Hair Trigger Built to be extremely reactive, this weapon will always win a priority order tie, and you gain advantage for this weapon for the first attack on the same turn it was drawn.
        Fully Automatic These are firearms that can fire rapidly without releasing the trigger, and they have magazines of ammo that can be reloaded when empty. Full-auto firearms will fire multiple times within a single action. The amount they fire per action is known as the SPA (shots per action), however firing a fully automatic weapon will always consume an entire action. As a rule, full-auto firearms will have a SPA of 3 to 4, but some specific weapons will differ and the specific fire rate will be detailed in that firearm's Item Description.
        Burst Fire Burst fire weapons are similar to full-auto where they will fire multiple shots in a single action, and therefore are included when you learn the Full-Auto skill. The difference between full-auto and burst is that the burst guns can fire a few rounds with a single trigger pull. These weapons will have similar SPA to full-auto firearms, and will take a full action to use. Unlike full-auto guns, burst fire tends to be much more accurate. Standard burst weapons will have an SPA of 3.
        Semi Automatic These are firearms that can fire as quickly as you can pull the trigger, they also have reloadable magazines. They tend to be more accurate than their fully automatic brothers. If you purchase a Semi-Automatic Firearm Skill you will also be able to use full-auto firearms with a Semi-Auto skill, however you will use it at Disadvantage. Unmodified or altered Semi-Automatic Firearms will have an SPA of 2; firing a Semi-Automatic Firearm once is a limited action.
        Manual Action These firearms include Bolt-Action, Pump-Action, Lever-Action, and Single-Action. They are guns that need to be manually operated in order to continue firing. They can, but do not always have reloadable magazines. These weapons are held high for their reliability and effectiveness. Manual Action firearms will have an SPA of 1, unless the weapon has been improved or modified in some way.
        Single Fire These firearms only fire one shot before needing to be reloaded. They are very reliable, and often powerful, but can consume time to use. Single Fire guns have an SPA of 1, but they will require another action to reload before firing again.

        Tag Description
        High Capacity Increases the ammunition capacity of the firearm's magazine.
        Heavy Barrel Increases weapon weight but reduces recoil and improves accuracy.
        Choked Tightens shotgun pellet spread pattern, extending effective range.
        Bipod / Tripod / Mounted The shooter can choose to deploy their mount as a full action and gain advantage on all rolls for this weapon, as long as they don't move, in which case the mount has been collapsed and must be redeployed for use.
        Suppressor / Silencer Eliminates the muzzle report of the firearm, firing this weapon while hidden will not reveal your position to enemies further than 3m away. Unless this weapon also has subsonic any enemy within 3m of the bullets flight path will hear the snap of a bullet passing by and know the shot was fired and the general direction it came from.
        Short Barrel Reduces weapon length and weight, improving close quarters handling but reducing velocity/range.
        Long Barrel Increases velocity and range, but increases overall weapon weight and size.
        Vertical Grip Improves vertical recoil control.
        Angled Grip Slightly improves handling speed and horizontal recoil control.
        Canted Grip Allows mounting offset sights for faster target acquisition.
        Folding Stock Allows folding the stock to reduce the weapon's inventory space profile.
        1-nX Optic Variable magnification scope providing flexibility for medium to long ranges.
        1x Optic Non-magnified red dot/holographic sight for fast close-range aiming.
        nX Optic Fixed magnification scope designed for dedicated long-range engagement.
        Flashlight Provides illumination in dark environments, potentially blinding targets at close ranges.
        Laser Improves hip-fire accuracy and target acquisition speed.

              System Tutorial
              • EDITING DATA: Click on any underlined text (like your Name or Player ID) to type directly into the sheet. Changes automatically save.
              • ADJUSTING STATS: Use the ▲ and ▼ arrows to scale your Mental and Physical characteristics between 1 and 20.
              • DEPENDANT CHARACTERSITICS: Click the ▲ arrows next to HP, STUN, RES, or RCT to buy extra levels with XP. Note that these are permanent and cannot be reduced (clicking ▼ will not decrease them).
              • HEALING HP/STUN: Current values are shown in Yellow. Base values are Blue. Click the blue base value to instantly reset your current HP or STUN back to full.
              • SKILLS & ABILITIES: Fill out the mini forms to add entries. Click or hover over them to view their notes.
              • DELETING ENTRIES: Click [DELETE ITEM] inside the expanded notes of Skills, Abilities, Inventory, or Equipped Gear to quickly remove the entry.
              • INVENTORY SLOTS: Base slots equal your STR + 2 (hands are included). If you equip a storage item, put its extra capacity into the "+ MAX SLOTS" box to increase your overall carrying limit.
              • ITEM SIZES: Like items automatically pool slots together. Two "Small (1/2)" medkits will combine to fit into a single slot.
              • REFERENCE TABLES: Toggle the [ REFERENCE TABLES ] panel at the bottom to view the game tables arranged in optimized columns. Select between STATS & COSTS, COMBAT & RANGE, and TAGS REFERENCE tabs.
              • CRT THEMES: Toggle the drop-down selector at the top right to switch between Cyan CRT, Amber CRT, Green CRT, B&W CRT, and WW2 Notebook styles.
              • OPERATIVE KIA: Use the [ MARK KIA ] button to lock editing on a character record, and [ REVIVE ] to restore active status.
